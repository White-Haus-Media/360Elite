His Story

Built Different

Tyrell Grant Jr. was raised in a stable, supportive environment where discipline, work ethic, and character were lived, not just talked about. His parents, uncles, and close mentors shaped him into the man he is today, instilling lessons about accountability and how to carry himself on and off the field. With his father serving in the military, additional mentors stepped in to reinforce the importance of resilience.

His introduction to football came at age five, when his father recognized his natural energy and movement. Starting at offensive tackle, TJ built an early foundation for understanding the physical nature of the game before transitioning to safety, where his physicality, awareness, and competitive mindset found their true home.

"I hate losing in anything. That drives me to put in the work necessary to avoid that outcome. Pain is temporary. I take advantage of every opportunity." TJ Grant Jr.

His recruiting journey required patience and persistence. Despite consistent effort, TJ often felt overlooked, which only strengthened his resolve. A defining moment came when he decommitted from Syracuse University and committed to Virginia Tech just hours before signing day, a decision that required faith, trust, and conviction. Off the field, TJ is pursuing a degree in Biomedical Science with plans to become an anesthesiologist, proving his discipline extends far beyond the gridiron.
